Plot of Why Women Kill

The plot of the series is built around the theme of marital infidelity and how betrayal can lead to murder.

Season 1: Three Women, One House

The first season tells about women living in one mansion at three different times:

  1. 1963 (Beth Ann): An exemplary housewife discovers that her husband is having an affair with a waitress. Trying to save the marriage, she begins to secretly be friends with her husband's mistress, but gradually realizes the true nature of her husband.
  2. 1984 (Symona): The socialite finds out that her husband is gay. While she tries to save face in front of society and plans a divorce, she has an affair with the 18-year-old son of her best friend.
  3. 2019 (Taylor): A successful lawyer is openly married. Problems begin when she brings her lover Jade into the house, with whom her husband eventually falls in love.

The action takes place in the same mansion in Pasadena, but in three different decades. Each heroine learns about her husband's infidelity, which triggers a chain of events leading to a fatal outcome.

Season 2: Price of recognition

The action is transferred to 1949. The plot focuses on one timeline and new characters.

  • Alma Fillcot: A modest housewife whose main dream is to get into the prestigious garden club headed by the glamorous Rita Castillo.
  • The husband's secret: Alma accidentally learns that her husband Bertram (a good-natured veterinarian) is a serial killer who "out of mercy" euthanizes terminally ill people.
  • Transformation: Instead of betraying her husband, Alma begins to use his methods to hide her secrets and pave the way to social success, gradually turning from a victim into a dangerous manipulator.
