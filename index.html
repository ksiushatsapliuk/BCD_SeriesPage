About the series "Why Women Kill"

Why Women Kill is a black comedy drama created by Mark Cherry , which in an anthology format tells the stories of various women who faced marital infidelity and the events that led to the murders.

Series intro

Season 1 (2019):
Set in three different time periods (1963, 1984, and 2019) within a Pasadena mansion, the series follows housewife Beth Ann Stanton (Ginnifer Goodwin), socialite Simone Grove (Lucy Liu), and attorney Harding (Kirby Howell-Baptiste), each of whom finds themselves in the midst of a teenage rainbow.
Season 2 (2021):
Set entirely in 1949 Los Angeles , the series focuses on a single story about shy housewife Alma Fillcott (Allison Tolman) who aspires to join an elite garden club and its president, Rita Castillo (Lana Parria). The season explores notions of beauty and the limits a woman will go to in order to fit in.
Season 3 Cancellation:
The series was quite popular and even ranked in the top 10 series on the Paramount+ platform, but despite this, the streaming service made the "difficult decision" not to continue production of a third season, the reasons for which were not publicly disclosed.

The television series "Why Women Kill" belongs to the genres of black comedy, drama, and crime thriller.

More about genres

The series combines several genre elements to create its unique tone:

  • Dark Comedy: This is the main genre of the series. The humor arises from horrific or serious situations, such as betrayal and murder, which are central to the plot.
  • Drama: The series delves deeply into the lives of three (in the first season) or one (in the second season) women, exploring their emotional experiences, relationships, disappointments, and reactions to their husbands' betrayals.
  • Crime Thriller: Since the plot of each season ultimately leads to murder, there are elements of criminal investigation, intrigue, and suspense that are typical of thrillers.

    • All these genres create the unique athmosphere of show.

Overall, the series can be described as an anthology with elements of a soap opera, using these genres to explore themes of marital infidelity, revenge, and social expectations in different historical eras.
